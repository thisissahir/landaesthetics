# LAND AESTHETICS
## Brand Brief — meaning, exact system, constraints, amplification

*Governing rule: this brand must never look templated. If an asset could belong to any other med spa, it is wrong. The test for every piece: would this hang in a gallery, or does it look like a GlossGenius default? Only the first passes.*

---

### 1. THE MEANING

Land Aesthetics treats skin as a canvas and Marjorie as the artist who restores it. That is not decoration, it is the truth of the work: this is a paramedical, corrective practice that fixes what other spas cannot, tattoos, scars, melasma, pigment, texture, damage. Science with an artist's eye.

The painterly face on the homepage says everything the brand is before a single word: expressive, human, unmistakably not a template. Every competitor hides behind cream and gold. Land shows up as art.

**What it stands for:** the artistry of correction. The hardest skin problems, solved by a specialist who sees skin the way a painter sees a canvas.

**One line:** Your skin is the canvas. I restore it.

**Positioning:** Denver's skin fixer. The paramedical specialist you go to when it actually has to work.

---

### 2. THE EXACT SYSTEM  (definition)

The world is a quiet gallery wall holding one bold piece of art. Restraint around the frame, richness in the work. Editorial, not spa. The discipline is what makes it read as expensive.

**Exact rules (this is what keeps it un-templated):**
- The frame is always Dusty Rose plus Near-Black serif. That pairing is the brand signature. Hold it on every surface.
- The jewel tones are **art, not interface**. They live inside imagery and painterly elements, never as button colors, never as body text. The moment they become UI, the gallery becomes a template.
- Text is Near-Black on Dusty Rose, or white on a dark artwork. Both clear contrast. Nothing else.
- Minimum body 16px.

**Type**
- Display: a wide-set, high-contrast fashion serif (the existing LAND AESTHETICS wordmark voice). Generous letterspacing. This is the gallery label.
- Body: one clean, quiet sans. Lots of air.
- **No script.** Deliberately. Script is what every other spa uses. Land's restraint is its sophistication. Two type voices, maximum, ever.

**Texture and light**
Painterly, tactile, real. Canvas texture, brushstroke, natural light on real skin. When photography appears, it is styled like an editorial, not a phone dump. Space is the luxury.

### 3. CONSTRAINTS, GUARDRAILS, AND AMPLIFICATION

**Anti-template constraints (non-negotiable, this is the brief's whole point)**
- **The gallery frame is always visible.** Dusty Rose ground plus black serif on every asset. If a post could be lifted onto any other spa's page unchanged, it has failed.
- **Kill the sticker chaos.** Retire the five-color Canva caption labels (blue, yellow, pink, green). One caption treatment: black or white type, one placement, editorial. The current feed fights the website. The feed loses.
- **Before/afters are gallery plates, not clinical dumps.** Consistent framing, consistent lighting, one clean label, on the Dusty Rose ground. Same proof, styled like art. This alone separates Land from every before/after farm in the city.
- **No generic stock, ever.** Replace GlossGenius default thumbnails with real, on-brand imagery. Stock is the tell of a template.
- **One identity.** Unify Land Aesthetics and the Marjorie handle into one name and one lockup across every surface.

**Amplification (lean hard into what already works)**
1. **The art direction is the moat.** No other Denver med spa looks like a gallery. Push it further, do not sand it down. This is the reason someone remembers Land and forgets the cream-and-gold crowd.
2. **Marjorie the educator.** Her teaching content (skin anatomy, laser science, honest product talk, "let's talk about burns") is real authority that converts at full price. Make it a named, recurring series in the gallery system. Authority beats discounts.
3. **Own the correction niche.** Lead with the hardest problems she solves, tattoo removal, scars, melasma, resurfacing. Specialists do not compete on price. "Denver's skin fixer" is the whole growth engine.

**GUARDRAILS (compliance, non-negotiable)**
- Corrective and laser claims (tattoo removal, resurfacing, melasma, pigment) are regulated. Avoid absolutes: "erase your tattoo" overpromises, since removal is often fade and multi-session with variable results. Say "fade," "clear," "sessions," and set expectations.
- Every before/after needs written consent, consistent conditions, and a "results vary, multiple sessions typical" note.
- Educational content on burns and adverse reactions is a strength, but frame it as education and safety, never as a scare tactic or a claim to treat medical conditions.
- Verify all of the above with healthcare-marketing counsel.
